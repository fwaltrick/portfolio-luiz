description_en: >
  `The Pinacoteca de São Paulo` (often simply referred to as **Pinacoteca**),
  one of Brazil's leading art museums, is housed in a historic building dating
  back to 1900, located in the city center. Founded in 1905, it underwent a
  major renovation in the 1990s under the direction of renowned architect Paulo
  Mendes da Rocha. Since then, it has evolved into a dynamic cultural
  institution that actively engages in the international exhibition scene and
  organizes numerous cultural events. The museum’s collection, comprising more
  than ten thousand works, primarily showcases the history of Brazilian painting
  from the 19th and 20th centuries.


  I had the opportunity to contribute to the development of visual communication
  for over 50 temporary exhibitions at the museum.
